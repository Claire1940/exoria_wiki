## Getting Started: The Raid House

All raiding activity in Exoria begins at the **Raid House**. This central hub is where you will find the necessary NPCs to form a party, adjust your difficulty settings, and ultimately claim your rewards. Before jumping into a raid, ensure you have used any available referral codes, such as `::ref ends`, which often provide daily mystery boxes to help supplement your supplies.

To start a raid, interact with the Raid Manager NPC. You can choose to enter solo or create a party for a group encounter. The difficulty of the raid scales based on the number of players and the "Invocations" you select before starting the instance.

## Argus Raid Mechanics Overview

The Argus Raid is a "tri-brid" encounter, meaning you must be prepared to use Melee, Ranged, and Magic attacks. If you attempt to use only one style, you will find yourself unable to damage the boss during specific phases, leading to an inevitable failure. 

### The Style Switching Cycle
Argus will periodically change his defensive stance. During these transitions, he may become completely immune to Melee and Ranged attacks. This is often referred to as the "Shielded" or "Sleeping" phase.

> **⚠️ Warning:** When Argus enters his shielded state, Melee and Ranged weapons will hit constant zeros. You **must** switch to Magic (such as the Hellfire Barrage or Affliction spells) to break through his defenses and continue the fight.

### Prayer Flicking and Protection
Argus utilizes all three combat styles against the player. Watching the boss's animations is vital for survival.
*   **Magic Attack:** Often characterized by a distinct projectile or magical glow.
*   **Ranged Attack:** High-speed projectiles that can hit through standard armor.
*   **Melee Attack:** Occurs when the boss is in close proximity or performs a heavy physical animation.

| Boss Attack Style | Required Protection Prayer | Visual Cue |
|---|---|---|
| **Magic** | Protect from Magic | Blue/Purple energy build-up |
| **Ranged** | Protect from Missiles | Green/Swift projectile movement |
| **Melee** | Protect from Melee | Physical lunge or stomp |

## Essential Gear Setup

For a successful run as outlined in this **exoria raid guide**, your inventory management is just as important as your combat skills. You need enough space for gear switches without sacrificing too many slots for food and potions.

### Recommended Loadout
1.  **Melee Set:** Your primary DPS for the main phases. High strength bonus gear is preferred.
2.  **Magic Switch:** A powerful staff (like the Hellfire Staff) and magic robes. Do not forget to set your Auto-Cast to a high-level spell like Affliction.
3.  **Potions:** Overloads, Prayer Renews, and Saradomin Brews. A "Bottomless Saradomin Brew" is highly recommended for sustained healing.
4.  **Spec Weapon:** Weapons that lower the boss's defense can significantly speed up the kill time.

## Environmental Hazards: Tornadoes and Storms

During the fight, Argus will summon "Storms" or "Tornadoes" that move across the arena. These are not merely visual effects; staying inside a storm will deal rapid, stacking damage that can deplete your health in seconds.

*   **Movement is Key:** Constantly reposition yourself to avoid the path of the tornadoes.
*   **Don't Corner Yourself:** Try to stay toward the center of the arena so you have multiple directions to escape when a storm spawns.
*   **Priority:** It is often better to stop attacking for a moment to move out of a storm than to try and "tank" the damage while finishing a hit.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "True Tile" highlight if your client supports it. This helps you see exactly where your character is standing, making it easier to dodge the edges of the storm clouds.

## The Invocation System

The Invocation system allows players to customize the raid's difficulty. Each invocation adds a "Level" to the raid. Higher total levels increase the hitpoints and damage of Argus but significantly boost the quality of the loot from the final chest.

| Invocation Name | Effect | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **On a Diet** | Restricts certain food items or healing effectiveness. | Rely more on Saradomin Brews and overhead prayers. |
| **Empty** | Potions may automatically deactivate or become unusable. | Focus on fast kills to minimize the need for long-term buffing. |
| **Deadly Prayers** | Prayer drain is increased or missed flicks deal more damage. | Bring extra Prayer Potions or Restores. |
| **Tornado Fiesta** | Increases the number of storms in the arena. | Requires near-constant movement and high focus. |

For beginners, it is recommended to start with a Raid Level of 0 to 20 to learn the mechanics. Once you can consistently clear the raid in under 5 minutes, begin adding invocations to reach Level 30 and beyond for better rewards.

## Loot and Rewards

After defeating Argus, you will be teleported to the reward room where a chest awaits. The loot is generated based on your performance, the number of participants, and the total Invocation level.

Common rewards include:
*   **Scory Ars:** Useful for various high-level crafting and upgrades.
*   **Raw Cash:** Typically ranging from 2M to 6M+ per run.
*   **Crystallic Whip:** A rare and powerful melee weapon that is highly sought after by players.
*   **Crystal Shards:** Used for charging specialized gear.

Consistent raiding is the most reliable way to build wealth in Exoria. A standard solo run without heavy invocations usually takes between 4 and 5 minutes, while a coordinated duo can easily push for sub-3-minute times. For more information on official drop rates, you can visit the [Official RSPS Communities](https://www.runescape.com) or the Exoria community forums.

## FAQ

**Q: Why am I hitting zeros on Argus even with high-tier Melee gear?**

A: Argus has a specific phase where he is immune to Melee and Ranged damage. When this happens, you must switch to your Magic gear and use spells like Affliction to continue dealing damage.

**Q: What is the best invocation for a beginner to use in an exoria raid guide?**

A: "On a Diet" is often considered the most manageable invocation for beginners, as it primarily requires you to be more mindful of your healing sources rather than adding complex new mechanics to the fight.

**Q: Can I solo the Argus Raid?**

A: Yes, the Argus Raid is fully soloable. However, you must be very proficient at switching your protection prayers and dodging the storms, as the boss will focus all of its attacks on you.

**Q: How do I get to the Raid House?**

A: You can use the teleport menu or a direct command if available (check the in-game `::commands` list). The Raid House contains all the NPCs needed to start the Argus and Faceless King raids.

**Q: Is the Crystallic Whip a guaranteed drop?**

A: No, the Crystallic Whip is a rare drop from the reward chest. To increase your chances of obtaining it, you should increase your Raid Level by enabling more Invocations.
