## Economic Strategies for Clan Growth

The Exoria economy is notoriously robust. With individual prizes in community events often hitting the "50 bill" mark, clans must have a strategy for wealth distribution. Solo players often struggle to reach these milestones, but a coordinated **exoria clan guide** strategy can bridge that gap through "Split" systems.

### Loot Splitting vs. Keepers

1.  **Loot Splitting:** All high-value drops are sold, and the gold is divided equally among participants. This is the fairest method for casual clans.
2.  **The "Tank" Bonus:** Giving a small percentage extra to the player who took the most damage or provided the most utility (e.g., healing).
3.  **FFA (Free For All):** Whoever sees the loot first keeps it. This is generally discouraged in professional clans as it breeds toxicity.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I find the best clan for my playstyle?**

A: The best way to find a group is to hang out in high-traffic areas like the home bank or the Grand Exchange. Most clans advertise their recruitment threads on the official forums or Discord. Look for a group that matches your activity level and goals.

**Q: What is the "50 bill" event mentioned in the exoria clan guide?**

A: This refers to massive community giveaways often hosted during holidays or server milestones. These events are designed to inject wealth back into the community and provide new players with a chance to jumpstart their progression.

**Q: Is it possible to run a clan entirely solo?**

A: While you can technically hold the "Owner" rank alone, a clan requires at least 5-10 active members to be functional for events and chat-based perks. A "solo clan" is mostly used for private storage and is not recommended for social players.

**Q: How do I handle a member who steals from the clan bank?**

A: Immediately remove the player and add them to the server-wide "Blacklist." In 2026, most reputable clans share a shared database of known scammers to protect the community at large. Always use rank-based permissions to prevent low-ranking members from accessing valuable assets.
