## Choosing the Best Race for Your Mage

In Exoria, your choice of race provides the foundation for your entire progression. For those pursuing a dedicated **exoria mage build**, selecting a race that offers flat magic damage bonuses is non-negotiable. Certain races provide a 10% magic damage boost right from level one, which stacks multiplicatively with your equipment.

| Race Type | Magic Bonus | Physical Bonus | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Elf / Caster Variant** | **+10% Magic Damage** | -5% Health | Pure DPS Builds |
| **Human** | +5% All Damage | Balanced Stats | Hybrid Builds |
| **Orc / Brute** | -10% Magic Damage | +15% Health | Not Recommended for Mages |

By starting with a race that favors magic, you ensure that every point of Intelligence you invest later on yields higher returns. This 10% base bonus is the difference between needing twelve fireballs to kill a boss and needing only nine.

## Optimizing Your Exoria Mage Build Stats

The primary stat for any spellcaster is Intelligence (INT). However, many players make the mistake of ignoring their secondary needs, leading to a build that runs out of mana too quickly or dies in a single hit. To optimize your **exoria mage build**, you must balance your raw damage output with enough utility to stay mobile.

### Stat Priority Ranking

1.  **Intelligence (INT):** Increases total mana and magic damage scaling.
2.  **Agility:** Improves casting speed and movement, which is vital for kiting.
3.  **Vitality:** Provides a small buffer for mistakes, though it should be your lowest priority.

> **💡 Tip:** As a mage, you should never be in melee range. If you find yourself taking damage frequently, invest in Agility rather than Vitality to improve your dodging capabilities.

| Level Range | Recommended INT | Recommended AGI | Focus Area |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 - 10** | 20 - 30 | 5 - 10 | Basic Spell Scaling |
| **11 - 20** | 40 - 60 | 15 - 20 | Mana Pool Expansion |
| **21 - 40** | 80+ | 30+ | Maximize DPS Output |

## Essential Skills and Grimoires

The heart of the **exoria mage build** lies in the Grimoire system. A Grimoire is not just a weapon; it is a massive stat stick that can provide up to a 20% boost to your magic damage. When combined with the 10% racial bonus, you are looking at a 30% increase before even considering your base stats.

### Key Skills to Unlock
*   **Fireball:** Your bread-and-butter projectile. It is efficient and has a decent travel speed.
*   **Advanced Firewall:** This is a crucial skill for bossing. It deals massive damage over time and can put bosses into a "vulnerable" state.
*   **Mana Shield:** A defensive necessity that uses your mana pool to absorb incoming damage.

## Soloing Bosses: The Godwyn Speedrun Strategy

Soloing Godwyn is a rite of passage for any player using an **exoria mage build**. While other classes might struggle with his high physical resistance, a mage can exploit his vulnerability to fire.

1.  **Preparation:** Ensure you have your 20% Magic Damage Grimoire equipped.
2.  **The Vulnerable State:** Use **Advanced Firewall** to chip away at his stance.
3.  **Physical Transition:** Even as a mage, you may need to land a single physical hit with a spear or staff to ensure you receive full XP credit for the kill.
4.  **The Finish:** It typically takes 9 to 10 well-placed fireballs to finish Godwyn if your magic damage is optimized at level 15-20.

## Equipment and Gear Progression

Aside from the Grimoire, your armor choices should focus on mana regeneration and cooldown reduction. In the current 2026 version of the game, "Cloth" tier armor is the standard for the **exoria mage build**, as it provides the highest Intelligence bonuses.

*   **Headgear:** Wizard's Hat (increases mana regen).
*   **Robes:** Sage's Robes (reduces skill cooldowns by 10%).
*   **Accessories:** Rings that provide flat +Magic Damage are superior to those that provide +Health.

> **Warning:** Avoid Heavy Armor. While the defense looks tempting, the "Weight" penalty will drastically slow down your mana regeneration, effectively neutering your damage output.

## Leveling Tips for 2026

To level your **exoria mage build** as quickly as possible, focus on mob density. Mages excel at Area of Effect (AoE) damage. Instead of fighting one enemy at a time, pull 3-4 enemies, group them together, and use your Firewall to burn them down simultaneously.

1.  **Levels 1-10:** Farm the forest goblins using basic Fireball.
2.  **Levels 11-20:** Head to the Godwyn ruins. Even if you can't solo the boss yet, the minions provide excellent XP.
3.  **Levels 21+:** Transition to the Elemental Planes where your magic resistance will help you survive against other casters.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best secondary stat for an exoria mage build?**

A: Agility is the best secondary stat. It allows you to move faster and cast spells more quickly, which is essential for the "hit and run" tactics required for high-level solo play.

**Q: Can I solo Godwyn at level 10?**

A: It is possible for highly experienced players, but it is generally recommended to wait until level 15 or 18. At these levels, your mana pool is large enough to sustain the 9-10 fireballs required to end the fight.

**Q: Does the Grimoire bonus stack with race bonuses?**

A: Yes. If your race gives a 10% bonus and your Grimoire gives a 20% bonus, these effects combine to significantly amplify your base magic damage, making your spells hit much harder than a standard build.

**Q: Which spell is better: Fireball or Advanced Firewall?**

A: Both serve different purposes. Fireball is better for single-target sniping and finishing off enemies, while Advanced Firewall is superior for bossing and clearing large groups of mobs due to its damage-over-time (DoT) effect.
