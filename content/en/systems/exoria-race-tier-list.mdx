## S-Tier: The Apex Predators

### Golem
The Golem currently sits at the top of the **exoria race tier list** for players who prefer a "juggernaut" playstyle. With the highest base health and physical resistance in the game, Golems are the only race that can reliably solo high-level mobs without a dedicated healer. 

To maximize the potential of a Golem, you will need to interact with Isaac at the town's potion shop. Isaac requires **Golemite**, an uncommon drop from Golem mobs located near the Spear Trainer. Golems are heavy hitters, but they move slowly. If you plan on using the Hammer Trainer in the Guild Hall, the Golem's strength modifiers will make you an unstoppable force in both PvP and PvE.

### Elf
Elves remain the undisputed kings of mana-based builds. Their high intelligence and mana regeneration make them the perfect choice for the Priest class or any build that utilizes magical abilities. In a world where healing is scarce, an Elf Priest is the most sought-after teammate for dungeon crawls. Their agility also allows them to kite dangerous enemies like Golems or Goblins with ease.

## A-Tier: Reliable and Versatile

### Human
Humans are the "jack-of-all-trades" in Exoria. While they don't excel in any one particular stat, they have no glaring weaknesses. Their most significant advantage is a bonus to experience gain, allowing Human players to reach level 20 and beyond faster than any other race. This makes them excellent for players who want to experiment with different trainers, such as switching from the Swordsman to the Dagger trainer without feeling penalized by their racial stats.

### Orc
Orcs are the masters of physical DPS. They lack the pure defensive capabilities of the Golem but make up for it with higher critical hit chances and raw attack power. An Orc paired with the Spear Trainer—located up the ladders past the Golem fields—is one of the most efficient ways to clear goblin camps. Their "Berserk" passive triggers at low health, providing a massive damage boost that can turn the tide of a difficult boss fight.

## B-Tier: Niche Specialists

### Goblin
Goblins are often overlooked on the **exoria race tier list**, but they serve a very specific purpose: economy. Goblins have a passive that increases gold drops from enemies and reduces the cost of items at shops. If you find yourself constantly short on gold for new armor or weapon upgrades, a Goblin build can help you stockpile resources quickly. However, their low health makes them very fragile in late-game combat, especially against the Golems near the Spear Trainer's location.

## Trainer Locations and Race Synergy

To truly utilize your race's potential, you must visit the correct trainers. Each trainer specializes in a weapon type that scales with specific racial stats.

1.  **Swordsman Trainer:** Located in the church to the right of the spawn area. Best for Humans and Orcs.
2.  **Dagger Trainer (Rogue):** Found behind crates near the Tavern. Best for Elves and Goblins due to high agility requirements.
3.  **Hammer Trainer:** Found inside the Guild Hall. Best for Golems and Orcs.
4.  **Spear Trainer:** Located past the Golem fields, up a series of ladders. Best for Orcs.
5.  **Fist/Akuma Trainer:** Located in a hut past the waterfall near the Goblin camp. Best for high-agility builds.

## How to Optimize Your Race Choice

When selecting a race, consider your long-term goals. If you enjoy solo play, the Golem's durability is unmatched. If you prefer playing in a group, the Elf's utility is mandatory. For those who are undecided, the Human's balanced stats provide the most flexibility. 

> **💡 Tip:** Before committing to a race, visit the [official Exoria: World's End page](https://www.roblox.com/games/) to check for any recent patch notes regarding racial ability reworks.

### Farming for Race Progression
Many races require specific items to reach their "Awakened" states. Golems, for example, must farm Golemite. Golems are found in a specific clearing near the Spear Trainer. They do a lot of damage and have high health, so it is highly recommended to bring a friend or be at least level 20 before attempting to farm them. Once you have Golemite, take it to Isaac at the potion shop in town to complete his delivery quest.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the absolute best race in the Exoria race tier list for beginners?**

A: For beginners, the Human race is highly recommended. The experience boost helps you level up faster, and the balanced stats allow you to try out all the different trainers (Sword, Dagger, Spear, etc.) without having to reroll your character.

**Q: How do I get Golemite for the Golem quest?**

A: Golemite is a drop from Golem mobs. You can find Golems by following the path past the town, taking a left at the fork, and heading toward the ladders that lead to the Spear Trainer. They are difficult to kill, so consider grouping up.

**Q: Can I change my race later in the game?**

A: Yes, you can change your race using Race Rerolls, which are typically available through the in-game shop or as rare rewards from events. However, rerolling is random, so you may not get your desired race on the first try.

**Q: Where is the Fist (Akuma) trainer located?**

A: The Fist trainer is located in a small hut past the Goblin camp. You need to cross a bridge over a waterfall and follow the trail to the right to find his secluded location. This trainer is excellent for high-agility races like Elves or Goblins.
