Exoria is a uniquely challenging Minecraft modpack that transports players to a desolate, volcanic planet far from the comforts of the Overworld. Created by the mastermind behind *Age of Engineering*, this pack emphasizes a scripted progression system where every resource must be fought for and every mechanic understood. This **exoria wiki** guide is designed to help you navigate the initial hours of exile, ensuring you don't succumb to the toxic atmosphere or the literal acid rain that plagues the surface. Whether you are a veteran of expert-style modpacks or a newcomer looking for a thematic survival experience, using this **exoria wiki** resource will provide the strategic edge needed to reclaim your place in the universe.

## Surviving the First Day: Surface Gathering

Upon spawning in Exoria, you are immediately greeted by a landscape of ash, basalt, and flowing lava. Your first priority is to follow the Quest Book, which serves as your primary guide through the pack’s complex progression. Unlike standard Minecraft, you cannot simply punch trees to get wood; in fact, there are no trees to be found initially. You must scavenge the surface for debris left behind by the planet's harsh conditions.

### Essential Starting Materials

During the daylight hours, focus on picking up item piles scattered across the ground. These represent the remnants of the world that once was.

| Material | Primary Source | Initial Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Flint** | Surface Piles | Used with Rocks to create fire starters. |
| **Bone** | Surface Piles / Corpses | Essential for early-game bonemeal and tools. |
| **Rock** | Surface Piles | Can be thrown as a weapon or used for fire starting. |
| **Rotten Flesh** | Surface Piles | Can be dried or processed for basic sustenance. |
| **Ash Block** | Ground Excavation | Used for basic construction and crafting recipes. |
| **Basalt** | Underground / Lava Edges | A sturdy building block and component for early machinery. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid the "acid" or "poison" liquids found in craters. Touching these without proper protection will apply debilitating debuffs that can lead to a quick death in the early game.

## The Equal Trade Portal

One of the most critical mechanics you will encounter early on is the Equal Trade Portal. This is not a portal you walk through; rather, it is a gateway for exchanging the meager resources of the exiled planet for the biological necessities of life. If the portal appears inactive, you may need to "re-light" it using specific quest-related items or a Wand of the Force, though most players will find it functional upon arrival.

To use the portal, you must throw specific items into the shimmering field. The portal operates on a "one-to-one" or "set-to-set" exchange rate. For example, throwing in a specific number of bones or flint might return saplings or fertilizer.

### Early Game Portal Exchanges

| Item Input | Quantity | Item Output |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bones** | 4 | Dead Saplings |
| **Flint** | 2 | Fertilizer |
| **Rotten Flesh** | 4 | Soil / Dirt Variants |
| **Rocks** | 8 | Cobblestone |

Mastering these trades is the only way to obtain organic materials. Without saplings, you cannot produce wood, and without wood, your progression into the technical tiers of the pack will be impossible.

## Fire Starting and Illumination

In Exoria, darkness is more than just a nuisance; it is a death sentence. The pack often features custom lighting mechanics where standard torches do not come pre-lit. You will find "Unlit Torches" on the surface or through crafting, which must be manually ignited.

To create fire, you must use a **Rock** and a piece of **Flint**. By right-clicking a rock placed on the ground with flint, you can generate sparks to light nearby torches or campfires. This mechanic emphasizes the "primitive" start of the modpack and forces players to value their light sources.

1.  Place a **Rock** on a solid surface.
2.  Hold **Flint** in your main hand and right-click the Rock.
3.  Ensure your **Unlit Torches** are placed nearby to catch the spark.
4.  Alternatively, use a lit torch to ignite others in a chain reaction.

## Wood Production: The Chopping Block

Once you have successfully traded for saplings via the portal, you must grow them using the fertilizer provided by the quest rewards. However, obtaining planks is not as simple as placing a log in your crafting grid. Exoria utilizes the **Chopping Block** mod to simulate a more realistic woodworking process.

To get planks and sticks, you must:
*   Craft a **Chopping Block** (usually from stones or basalt in this pack).
*   Place a log on top of the block.
*   Use an axe (or a primitive tool) to strike the log repeatedly.
*   The log will first split into planks, and further strikes will produce sticks.

This process is slower than traditional Minecraft but is a staple of the expert-pack experience. It encourages players to automate wood processing as soon as possible.

## Environmental Hazards and Survival Tips

The atmosphere of Exoria is actively hostile. Beyond the monsters that spawn in the pitch-black caves, the weather itself can be your undoing. This **exoria wiki** section highlights the two biggest threats: Acid Rain and Toxic Atmosphere.

### Acid Rain
Occasionally, the sky will darken with a green hue, signaling the start of acid rain. If you are caught in this weather without a roof over your head, you will take constant poison damage. 

> **💡 Tip:** Always carry enough blocks to build a quick 3x3 shelter. Early in the game, a simple "Roof Over My Head" is a quest requirement for a reason—it is your only defense against the elements.

### Waystones and Exploration
Because the terrain is so treacherous, exploration is dangerous. Fortunately, Exoria includes the **Waystones** mod. You will find Waystones naturally generated in the world, often near abandoned structures or mana pools. Activating these allows for instant teleportation between discovered locations, though it may cost experience points or specific items depending on the configuration.

| Hazard | Effect | Mitigation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Acid Rain** | Poison and Hunger debuffs | Stay under solid blocks (roof). |
| **Toxic Liquid** | Instant damage / Wither | Avoid contact; use buckets to clear paths. |
| **Pitch Blackness** | Increased mob spawns / "Grue" effects | Maintain constant light with torches. |
| **Lava Flows** | Fire damage / Terrain destruction | Use Basalt to redirect flows. |

## Advanced Resource Trading: Exoria Wiki Insights

As you progress beyond the first few hours, your reliance on the portal will shift toward more complex materials. The **exoria wiki** community often notes that players who fail to automate their basic resource gathering early on struggle with the mid-game grind. 

Keep an eye on the "Misty World" mechanics if they are integrated into your version of the pack. This mod adds a layer of atmospheric pressure and air quality that requires specialized respirators. While not immediately relevant in the first thirty minutes, it becomes a hard gate for deeper exploration into the planet's lower layers.

Always check your Quest Book for "Detect" quests. These quests don't require you to turn in items but simply to have them in your inventory. They are excellent for unlocking new branches of the tech tree without losing your hard-earned materials.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get my first piece of wood in the Exoria modpack?**

A: You cannot find wood on the surface. You must gather bones and flint, trade them through the Equal Trade Portal for saplings and fertilizer, grow the tree, and then use a Chopping Block to process the logs into planks and sticks.

**Q: Why am I taking damage when it rains?**

A: This is Acid Rain, a core mechanic of the pack. To survive, you must be under a solid block. Even a single block directly above your head can stop the damage, but building a small base is highly recommended.

**Q: I lost my starting portal; can I make a new one?**

A: The starting portal is usually fixed at your spawn point or a specific quest location. If you lose track of it, look for the Waystone you activated near spawn. If the portal is truly broken, some versions of the pack allow you to re-light it using a Wand of the Force from the Botania mod.

**Q: What is the best way to find Basalt?**

A: Basalt is commonly found near the large lava lakes or slightly underground. According to the **exoria wiki** strategies, digging just a few layers below the ash surface will usually reveal large veins of basalt, which is much more durable for base building than ash blocks.
