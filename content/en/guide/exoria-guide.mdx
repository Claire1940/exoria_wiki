## Mastering the Train Event

The centerpiece of the latest update is the Train Event. This dynamic world event offers some of the highest rewards in the game but requires coordination and persistence to complete. Understanding the timing and the wave structure is vital for any player looking to climb the leaderboards in 2026.

### Event Schedule and Mechanics

The Train Event spawns every 30 minutes and remains active for a 15-minute window. This frequent rotation means players can easily integrate it into their farming sessions. The event is divided into three distinct waves, each progressively more difficult than the last.

| Feature | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Spawn Frequency** | Every **30 minutes** |
| **Event Duration** | **15 minutes** total |
| **Wave Count** | **3 Waves** of enemies |
| **Scaling** | Difficulty increases per wave |
| **Player Bonus** | More players lead to **faster clears** |

While the event can be attempted solo, the scaling mechanics favor group play. The more players present, the faster the waves are cleared, though the enemies will hit harder as you progress through the second and third stages.

> **💡 Tip:** Always check the server clock before entering a long dungeon. You don't want to be stuck in a boss fight when the Train Event begins.

## Ether Relics and the Merchant System

One of the most significant additions to the economy is the introduction of **Ether Relics**. This specialized currency is exclusive to the Train Event and is the only way to acquire high-tier accessories from the new Merchant NPC.

### How to Earn and Spend Ether Relics

Ether Relics are awarded upon the successful completion of all three waves of the Train Event. These relics are separate from standard coins and represent your prowess in defending the realm. Once you have accumulated enough, you can visit the Merchant to browse a rotating stock of exclusive items.

| Item Type | Acquisition Method | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ether Relics** | Complete **Train Event** waves | Currency for Merchant |
| **Exclusive Accessories** | Purchase with **Ether Relics** | Significant stat boosts |
| **Dungeon Loot** | Boss drops and chests | Can be **sold** to Merchant |
| **Coins** | Quests and selling items | General upgrades |

The Merchant also serves as a central hub for clearing out your inventory. You can now sell items obtained from various dungeons, including the difficult Boss Dungeon, to the Merchant in exchange for coins. This provides a consistent way to profit even if a specific gear drop isn't an upgrade for your current build.

## Quality of Life: Inventory and Stacking

For many players, inventory management was a major hurdle in earlier versions of the game. The 2026 updates have addressed this by making items stackable. This change allows you to carry significantly more materials and consumables without needing to constantly return to town or sell items mid-run.

This improvement is particularly helpful during the Train Event, where the sheer volume of loot can quickly overwhelm an unoptimized inventory. Combined with the ability to sell dungeon loot to the Merchant, the gameplay loop is now much smoother and more rewarding.

## Advanced Combat Strategies

To succeed in the later waves of the Train Event and the Boss Dungeons, you must master the combat system. Exoria relies on a mix of positioning, cooldown management, and elemental advantages.

*   **Wave Management:** In the Train Event, focus on area-of-effect (AoE) abilities during Wave 1 and Wave 2. For Wave 3, switch to high single-target damage to take down the elite enemies that spawn.
*   **Team Synergy:** If playing in a group, ensure at least one player is focused on crowd control. Keeping enemies grouped together allows the rest of the team to maximize their damage output.
*   **Resource Conservation:** Don't use all your powerful abilities at the start of a wave. Wait for the tougher "tank" enemies to appear before unleashing your ultimate skills.
