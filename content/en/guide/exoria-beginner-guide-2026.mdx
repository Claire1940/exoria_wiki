## Getting Started: Controls and Navigation

Before you can start purifying the streets, you must master the basic movement and interface of Exoria. The game utilizes a standard WASD movement scheme, but several unique keybinds are essential for high-level play. One of the first things you will notice is the inclusion of a hoverboard, which is your primary method of transportation across the sprawling map.

| Keybind | Action | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **3** | **Activate Hoverboard** | Summons your board for faster travel across the world. |
| **Z, X, C, V** | **Skills** | Activates your class-specific abilities and combat moves. |
| **B (Hold)** | **Grip** | Executes a "finisher" on downed enemies to gain XP and purity. |
| **Space (Double)** | **Dash/Jump** | Used for dodging attacks or reaching high platforms. |
| **M** | **Menu** | Accesses your inventory, quests, and character stats. |

> **Warning:** When using the side-dash, be aware of the "floaty" physics. Your character may continue to drift for a second after the dash animation ends, which can leave you vulnerable to enemy counter-attacks if timed poorly.

## Choosing Your Class and Initial Quests

Upon entering the Academy, you will be prompted to select a class. In the current 2026 meta, the two most prominent starting paths involve the Arya class (utilizing firearms) and the Demon class (utilizing blue flames and melee strikes). Each class offers a distinct playstyle:

*   **Arya Class:** Focuses on long-range engagement and tactical positioning. It is excellent for players who prefer to stay out of the fray while dealing consistent damage.
*   **Demon Class:** Heavily inspired by the series' protagonist, this class focuses on high-impact melee combos and crowd control through stunning effects.

Your first quest will typically involve defeating "Weak Demons" in the forest. This is the perfect time to practice your **Z Skill**, which for many classes acts as a primary stun or combo starter. The VFX in Exoria are particularly detailed, so pay attention to the visual cues of your skills to understand their effective range.

## The Purity System: Light vs. Shadow

One of the most defining features of Exoria is the Purity system. Your actions directly influence whether your character aligns with the Path of Light or the Path of Shadow. This is not just a cosmetic choice; it dictates which quests you can accept and how certain NPCs interact with you.

| Path | Primary Action | Alignment Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light (Positive)** | Purifying Wandering Spirits | Increases positive purity and grants bonus XP. |
| **Shadow (Negative)** | Slaying Exorcists | Increases negative purity and unlocks dark-aligned skills. |

When you encounter Wandering Spirits, you can "size" them to gain resonance. The rarity of the spirit determines the amount of XP and purity gained.

### Spirit Rarity Tiers
1.  **Common:** Frequent spawns, provides base XP.
2.  **Uncommon:** Slightly higher rewards, often found near the Academy outskirts.
3.  **Rare:** Significant XP boost, requires higher resonance levels to capture.
4.  **Legendary:** Extremely rare spawns that provide massive level jumps and purity shifts.

## Combat Mastery and the "Grip" Mechanic

Combat in Exoria is more than just mashing buttons. To successfully defeat enemies and progress through the **exoria beginner guide 2026** milestones, you must understand the "Grip" mechanic. When an enemy's health reaches zero, they will enter a "downed" state. To fully defeat them and claim your rewards, you must stand over them and hold the **B key**.

This mechanic is reminiscent of "finishers" in other high-stakes RPGs. Failing to grip an enemy may allow them to respawn or be revived by other NPCs/players in certain zones. Furthermore, skills like **Soul Conversion** (typically mapped to the X key) can bind an enemy's soul, leaving them completely unable to move or counter-attack for several seconds. This is vital when facing Elite Demons or Bosses who have high mobility.

> **💡 Tip:** Always use your stun skills (like the Z move) before attempting a Soul Conversion. This ensures the enemy cannot dodge the binding animation, which has a slightly longer wind-up time.

## Progression, Hoverboards, and Economy

As you complete daily quests and defeat demons, you will earn currency that can be spent at various shops. The most important early-game investment is your hoverboard. The base board is functional but slow; upgrading your board speed at the Skate Shop is essential for completing timed quests and traversing the map efficiently.

### Hoverboard Upgrade Costs (Estimated 2026)
*   **Level 1 (Default):** Free, Base Speed.
*   **Level 2 Upgrade:** 500 Credits, 15% Speed Increase.
*   **Level 3 Upgrade:** 1,200 Credits, 30% Speed Increase + Custom VFX.

Daily quests are another critical component of the economy. These quests often reward "Unlock Cards" or "Rerolls," which allow you to change your character's secondary traits or appearance without spending Robux. Always check the Daily Quest board near the Academy entrance first thing every session.

## Advanced Tips for Early Success

To truly excel in Exoria, you should keep several advanced strategies in mind. First, the "Chunk Loss" or "Rendering" settings can sometimes affect your ability to see distant spirits. If you are hunting for Legendary spirits, ensure your graphics settings allow for maximum draw distance.

Second, the knockback physics in the game can be frustrating. When you are hit by a heavy attack, you may feel like you are "floating" in slow motion. To mitigate this, try to use your dash immediately after the knockback starts to regain control of your character's momentum.

Finally, remember that you can only have one active quest tracked at a time. If you find a more lucrative quest, you may need to cancel your current one, though it is usually better to finish what you started to avoid losing progress on kill counts.

**Q: How do I get more skills in this exoria beginner guide 2026?**

A: Skills are unlocked by reaching specific level milestones (Level 6, 12, 15, and 30). You must also fulfill "Combat Mastery" requirements, such as using your primary skill a certain number of times or defeating specific types of demons.

**Q: What is the best way to farm XP?**

A: The most efficient way to farm XP is to hunt for Wandering Spirits while waiting for Elite Demon boss spawns. Legendary spirits provide the highest XP-to-time ratio in the game.

**Q: Why can't I stun certain bosses?**

A: High-level bosses and Elite Demons often have "Poise" or "Super Armor." You must deplete their invisible stamina bar with heavy attacks before your stun skills like Soul Conversion will take effect.
