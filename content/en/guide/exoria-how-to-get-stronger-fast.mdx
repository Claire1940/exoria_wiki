## Mastering Early Resource Processing

The first hurdle in Exoria is moving beyond basic hand-crafting. To accelerate your progress, you must construct a Flint Grindstone as soon as possible. This device allows you to double your output for materials like bone meal and fertilizer, which are critical for sustaining your food supply.

To create the grindstone, you will need to master the art of "Flaked Flint." Instead of using a crafting table, you must take raw flint and bang it against a hard surface (the ground) to produce a Flaked Flint Point. This is a recurring theme in Exoria: many early-game "recipes" involve physical interaction with the world rather than traditional UI crafting.

### Grindstone Crafting Requirements

| Component | Quantity | Source/Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wood Gears** | 2 | Crafted from sticks and planks |
| **Cobblestone** | 4 | Gathered from rock piles or generators |
| **Flaked Flint Point** | 1 | Bang Flint against the ground |
| **Wooden Crank** | 1 | Applied Energistics tool for manual power |

Once your grindstone is operational, use it to process bones into bone meal. This allows you to grow trees and crops instantly, which is the fastest way to gather the wood needed for base expansion and tool rods.

## Establishing a Sustainable Water Supply

In Exoria, water isn't as simple as finding an infinite spring. You must manufacture it using a Crucible. To get stronger, you need a steady supply of clay and water to create bricks and more advanced machinery.

The Crucible requires bricks, which are made by smelting clay. Since you are likely stranded on a planet with limited resources, you can obtain clay by "washing" Tear Clay in a water barrel. Once you have your Crucible, place a heat source (like a fire on an Ash block) beneath it. 

> **💡 Tip:** To fill a Crucible with water, you must physically drop leaves into the block. Oak leaves provide the best yield, but most non-dead leaf types will work.

### Water and Clay Production Steps

1.  **Craft a Wooden Barrel:** Use planks and slabs to create a basic container.
2.  **Wash Tear Clay:** Right-click Tear Clay into a water-filled barrel to get regular clay.
3.  **Smelt Bricks:** Use a basic furnace to turn clay into bricks for the Crucible.
4.  **Automate Heat:** Place fire on an Ash block; in Exoria, fire on Ash stays lit indefinitely, providing a permanent heat source for your Crucible.

## Exoria How to Get Stronger Fast: Unbreakable Tools

The absolute best way to increase your power level in the early game is to craft the "Hardened Stone" toolset. Unlike standard tools that break and require constant replacement, these specialized tools are incredibly durable and offer unique modifiers that speed up resource gathering significantly.

To unlock these, you first need to scavenge obsidian. Since you won't have a diamond pickaxe yet, you must use a Stone Hammer and "Shift + Right-Click" (sneak-scavenge) on obsidian blocks created from lava and water. This will yield Obsidian Shards, which can be smelted into plates.

### Essential Hardened Stone Tools

| Tool Name | Key Benefit | Why You Need It |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lumber Axe** | Instant Tree Felling | Chops down entire trees in a single hit, saving hours of woodcutting. |
| **Stone Pick** | Direct Inventory Deposit | Mined cobblestone goes directly into your inventory, preventing item loss. |
| **Hardened Shears** | High Durability | Essential for gathering the massive amounts of leaves required for water. |

These tools represent a massive leap in efficiency. The Lumber Axe, in particular, allows you to clear entire forests (or your own tree farms) in seconds, providing the charcoal and wood needed for high-level smelting.

## Upgrading to the Normal Furnace

You will notice that the "Basic Furnace" is slow and inefficient. To progress to a "Normal Furnace," Exoria requires you to build a specific environment. This is a unique quest-gate that many players miss. The furnace "needs a house" that is weatherproof, which involves using Wattle and Daub.

Wattle and Daub is crafted by combining Wicker (made from sugar cane or sticks) with Cob. You must replace the walls of your furnace room with these blocks to satisfy the quest requirements.

### Furnace Room Requirements

*   **Walls:** At least 40 blocks of Wattle and Daub or specialized Cob.
*   **Space:** The furnace must be placed within this enclosed, "weatherproof" structure.
*   **Benefit:** Unlocks standard smelting recipes and higher fuel efficiency, which is vital for **exoria how to get stronger fast**.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never attempt to expand your base or mine at night without proper lighting. The darkness in Exoria is inhabited by "The Grue," a creature that will kill you almost instantly if you stand in pitch-black areas.

## Advanced Farming and Trading

Once your basic survival is secured, focus on "Rummaging." By using a Seed Horn or rummaging through grass and dirt, you can find various saplings and seeds. These aren't just for food; they are your primary currency for "Elven Trading."

Trading charcoal and bamboo with the home planet allows you to receive Boreal Seeds and other exotic flora. This opens up the path to magic and more advanced resource generation.

1.  **Plant Bamboo:** It grows exceptionally tall and provides sticks and trading material.
2.  **Fruit Trees:** Orange, Cherry, and Apple saplings provide consistent food that restores more hunger than basic crops.
3.  **Fertilizer Doubling:** Surround your Fertilizer with Ash in a crafting grid to double your output, allowing for rapid crop growth.

## FAQ

**Q: Why am I moving so slowly on the ground in Exoria?**

A: The Ash that covers the planet has a movement penalty. To get around this, you should craft wooden paths or "Wattle" walkways. Walking on man-made blocks removes the slow-down effect, allowing you to traverse your base much faster.

**Q: How do I get obsidian without a diamond pickaxe?**

A: You don't mine it in the traditional sense. Craft a Stone Hammer and sneak (Shift) while right-clicking on an obsidian block. This "scavenges" the block, breaking it into Obsidian Shards which can then be processed into plates for advanced tools.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get bone meal?**

A: To understand **exoria how to get stronger fast**, you must use the Flint Grindstone. Processing a single bone in the grindstone with a wooden crank yields significantly more bone meal than standard crafting, which is essential for rapid tree farming.

**Q: Does the darkness actually kill you?**

A: Yes. Exoria features a "Grue" mechanic where being in total darkness for more than a few seconds results in a jumpscare and instant death. Always carry a light source or stay within your torch-lit base boundaries during the night cycles.
