## The Fish Tank Build: Resource Generation

In Exoria, the Fish Tank is not just a decorative piece; it is a functional machine used for breeding aquatic life that provides essential materials. Constructing a proper Fish Tank setup is one of the first major "tank" projects you will undertake. These tanks allow you to breed species like Pufferfish, which can be used in various alchemical and infusion recipes.

To get started, you will need to craft Fish Tanks using glass panes and buckets of water. Because these tanks function as breeding chambers, you often need to build them in large quantities to maximize output.

| Component | Quantity (Recommended) | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fish Tank Block** | 32 - 64 | The primary containment and breeding unit. |
| **Water Buckets** | 1 per tank | Required to initialize the aquatic environment. |
| **Seeds** | Varies | Used as bait/food to trigger the breeding process. |
| **Raw Fish** | 2+ per species | The starting "seed" population for your farm. |

> **💡 Tip:** When setting up your fish farm, place the tanks in a 2x2 or larger configuration. This allows the fish to move more freely and makes it easier to interact with them for breeding.

## Powering Your Infrastructure: Stirling Engines and BuildCraft

A robust **exoria tank build** requires a steady supply of power to run advanced machinery like the Assembly Table and Lasers. In the mid-game, you will likely transition to BuildCraft power (MJ) to facilitate the creation of Redstone Chipsets. These chipsets are the backbone of more advanced "sturdy" machinery.

The Stirling Engine is the most reliable entry-level power source. It accepts any fuel that a standard furnace would, such as charcoal or coal. However, managing the heat levels is crucial to prevent engine failure.

### Engine Setup Requirements
To run a five-laser Assembly Table setup efficiently, you should aim for a dedicated power room. This involves:
1.  **Stirling Engines:** At least 5–6 engines to maintain a steady MJ flow.
2.  **Kinesis Pipes:** Use Wooden Kinesis Pipes at the engine output and Cobblestone Kinesis Pipes for the main line.
3.  **Redstone Control:** Use a lever and redstone dust to toggle your engine array on and off to prevent overheating when not in use.

| Pipe Type | Max Flow (Approx) | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wooden Kinesis Pipe** | 16 MJ/t | Extracts power directly from the engine. |
| **Cobblestone Kinesis Pipe** | Unlimited (Modded) | Transports power to the Assembly Table. |
| **Quartz Kinesis Pipe** | High | Best for long-distance power transport with low loss. |

## Advanced Survival: The Life Infuser

Surviving the depths of Exoria requires items that cannot be simply crafted on a table. The Life Infuser is a multi-block structure from the Modular Machines mod that allows you to "infuse" life into mundane objects. This is how you obtain items like the Bucket of Cod or specialized survival gear.

Building the Life Infuser requires a specific layout of Rune Stone, Machine Controllers, and Tiny Input/Output ports.

### Building the Life Infuser Structure
*   **Machine Controller:** The "brain" of the structure, crafted from copper ingots.
*   **Tiny Item Inputs:** You will need three of these to feed the machine raw materials.
*   **Tiny Item Output:** One is sufficient for collecting the finished products.
*   **Rune Stone Bricks:** Used for the pillars and structural integrity.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Ensure the structure is built exactly according to the blueprint. Use the "Preview" function on the controller to see a ghost image of where blocks should be placed. Missing a single block will prevent the machine from forming.

## Processing Materials for a "Tanky" Defense

To truly achieve a "tank" status in Exoria, you must process raw ores into high-tier alloys. The Metal Press and the Stamper are your primary tools here. For example, creating Dawnstone Plates is essential for crafting "Sturdy Casings," which are used in almost every advanced forestry and industrial machine.

1.  **Melt the Ore:** Use an Ember-powered melter to turn Dawnstone or Copper into liquid form.
2.  **Extract the Fluid:** Use fluid extractors and pipes to move the liquid to a Stamper.
3.  **Stamp the Plate:** Place a plate mold in the Stamper to produce the necessary components for your **exoria tank build**.

| Machine | Input | Output | Power Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stamper** | Liquid Metal | Plates/Gears | Ember |
| **Assembly Table** | Redstone/Iron | Chipsets | MJ (BuildCraft) |
| **Squeezer** | Seeds/Fruit | Seed Oil/Juice | RF or MJ |

## Entering the Depths: The Exoria Portal

Once your resource tanks and power systems are established, the final stage of your early survival build is leaving the surface. The planetary surface eventually runs out of resources, forcing you to explore the caverns.

To do this, you must create the **Exoria Artifact**. This requires an Emerald (obtained via the Diamond Mesh sieve) and various crystals found in the world. Once the artifact is ready, construct a portal frame out of Rune Stone. This portal is your gateway to the next tier of the game, where the environment is significantly more hostile, making your previous work on your **exoria tank build** and armor even more critical.

You can find more detailed technical documentation on the [Official Exoria CurseForge page](https://www.curseforge.com/minecraft/modpacks/exoria) to help with specific mod interactions.

## Summary of Survival Steps
1.  **Automate Ash Processing:** Use Redstone Engines and chests to gather materials for Diamond Mesh.
2.  **Build the Fish Tank:** Secure a renewable source of fish for Life Infusion recipes.
3.  **Establish BuildCraft Power:** Set up Stirling Engines to craft Redstone Chipsets.
4.  **Construct the Life Infuser:** Use modular machine components to craft survival-tier items.
5.  **Activate the Portal:** Use the Exoria Artifact on a Rune Stone frame to descend into the caverns.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get Emeralds for the Exoria Artifact?**

A: You must process Dust or Ash through a Sieve with a **Diamond Mesh**. The drop rate is approximately 10%, so you will need to automate your ash collection to get enough materials.

**Q: My Stirling Engines are turning red. Are they going to explode?**

A: Yes, in many versions of BuildCraft, Stirling Engines can overheat and eventually explode if they are not cooled or turned off. Always use a redstone lever to shut down your **exoria tank build** power grid when your Assembly Table is idle.

**Q: What is the best way to breed fish in the Fish Tank?**

A: Place at least two fish of the same species in a Fish Tank and right-click them with Seeds. This will trigger the breeding mechanic, similar to how animals are bred in vanilla Minecraft.

**Q: Why isn't my Life Infuser working?**

A: Check that all three Tiny Item Inputs are present and that you have a Machine Controller. Also, ensure you are using the correct "Infusion Stone" (like the Sandstone Infusion Stone) inside the machine's interface.
