## Mastering the Controls and Mechanics

Before diving into the depths, you must familiarize yourself with the movement and combat system. Unlike many modern action games that use WASD for movement, Exoria defaults to the arrow keys on a keyboard. The game is highly optimized for a controller, but keyboard players can still excel by mastering a few specific keybindings.

| Action | Keyboard Input | Controller Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| **Movement** | Arrow Keys | Left Analog Stick |
| **Basic Attack** | Shift Key | X / Square |
| **Circular Attack** | Specialized Combo | Y / Triangle |
| **Dodge Roll** | Alt Key | B / Circle |
| **Grappling Hook** | Right-Click | RB / R1 |
| **Interact** | E Key | A / Cross |

One of the most important mechanics to note in this **exoria dungeon guide** is the "Slash-Forward" movement. Every time you perform a basic attack, your character lunges forward. While this is great for closing the gap on enemies, it is extremely dangerous near cliffs. Many players accidentally plummet to their deaths by attacking too close to a ledge.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always check your footing before initiating a combo. The forward momentum from slashing can easily carry you off a platform if you aren't careful.

## Early Dungeon Exploration: The First Augmentations

The early stages of the dungeon serve as a tutorial for combat and puzzle-solving. You will encounter rock-like enemies that emerge from the ground. These enemies are slow and can be dispatched with a simple three-hit combo. After clearing the first few rooms, keep an eye out for glowing items known as "Fragments d'Augmentation."

These fragments are vital for your survival as they increase your maximum health bar. You can track your progress via the icons located above your health bar. Collecting these early is the best way to prepare for the difficulty spikes in the later chambers.

### The Grappling Hook Acquisition

Progressing through the dungeon requires the Grappling Hook, which is found after navigating a series of hallways and clearing out shielded enemies. Shielded foes require you to "whack" their shields repeatedly until they break, or you can use the environment to your advantage.

Once you obtain the Grappling Hook (Right-Click), the game shifts into a more parkour-heavy experience. You can use the hook to pull yourself toward pillars or across large gaps.

| Puzzle Element | Interaction Method | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **Hanging Chains** | Slash with Sword | Opens nearby gates or doors |
| **Large Blocks** | Push/Grapple | Used to trigger wall switches or crush enemies |
| **Cracked Walls** | Explosive Enemies | Destroys the barrier to reveal new paths |

## Mid-Game Challenges: Bombs and Boxes

As you move deeper into the dungeon, the puzzles become more complex. You will encounter rooms filled with spawning boxes. These boxes aren't just for show; they are your primary tool for clearing obstacles. In the "Wall of Boxes" section, you must grapple and pull boxes into the wall to create a path or trigger a mechanism.

You will also meet "Bomb Guys"—enemies that explode upon death or after a short timer. These are both a threat and a tool. By kiting these enemies toward cracked walls, you can blow open new areas. However, be extremely cautious, as the explosion deals significant damage to your character.

## The Five-Door Hub Walkthrough

The climax of the dungeon takes place in a large central hub featuring five distinct doorways. Most of these are locked initially, requiring you to complete the challenges in the open rooms to unlock the others.

1.  **The Combat Room**: Focus on the simple sword-wielders first. Use the dodge roll (Alt) to get behind them.
2.  **The Bomb Chamber**: This room is dangerous. You must navigate stairs while Bomb Guys drop down. Try to hit them and immediately roll away to trigger their explosion without taking damage.
3.  **The Shield Gauntlet**: Here, you face a mix of shielded enemies and bomb throwers. Prioritize the bomb throwers, as their projectiles can stagger you, leaving you vulnerable to the shield-bearers.
4.  **The Augmentation Room**: Usually a platforming challenge involving the grappling hook. Success rewards you with a significant health boost.
5.  **The Boss Corridor**: Only opens once the previous four are cleared. Ensure you are at full health before entering.

> **💡 Tip:** There are often vases and pots hidden in the corners of the hub. Break these to find green "food" items that restore your health before you head into a new room.

## Boss Fight Strategy: Soldier Number 42

Soldier Number 42 (Soldat Numéro 42) is the final hurdle in the Exoria dungeon. This boss is a mirror of your own capabilities but with significantly more health and a larger reach. He utilizes a heavy shield and a sword that can drain your health in just a few hits.

### Phase 1: The Defensive Approach
Initially, the boss will be passive. Use this time to get in a few quick slashes while he is getting up. Once the fight begins in earnest, do not try to trade hits. He has a higher poise than you, meaning he won't stagger easily.

### Phase 2: Vulnerability Windows
Wait for Soldier 42 to perform a heavy overhead smash. This move has a long recovery time. Dodge to the side and land two or three hits before retreating. If you have any health pots left in the arena, save them for when you fall below 25% health.

### Phase 3: The Enraged State
When the boss reaches low health, he becomes more aggressive and can sprint toward you. Use your grappling hook to maintain distance if you need to heal. The key to winning this fight is patience; rushing in will lead to a quick defeat.

| Boss Move | Counter Strategy |
|---|---|
| **Shield Bash** | Roll backward; do not try to block |
| **Overhead Smash** | Side-step and punish during recovery |
| **Sprinting Slash** | Grapple to a nearby pillar to reset the distance |

Upon defeating Soldier 42, you will be prompted to pick up his sword. This triggers the final cutscene where the cycle continues, and your character undergoes a startling transformation.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Exoria available in English?**

A: As of 2026, the game is primarily in French. However, the gameplay is action-oriented, and the objectives are usually clear through visual cues, making it playable for non-French speakers.

**Q: How do I heal in the middle of a fight?**

A: Look for green glowing objects or breakable pots in the environment. These contain healing items. Note that some healing items may be glitched and won't trigger if you are currently in an attack animation.

**Q: What should I do if I get stuck on a platforming section in the exoria dungeon guide?**

A: Remember that you can influence your movement while grappling. Press the forward arrow key while being pulled by the grappling hook to gain extra momentum and reach farther platforms.

**Q: Is there fall damage in Exoria?**

A: Generally, there is no fall damage from standard heights. However, falling into a bottomless pit or off the main map will result in an instant death and will restart you at the last checkpoint.
