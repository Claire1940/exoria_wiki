## Initial Scanning and Scientific Curiosity

The first major hurdle in Exoria’s tech progression is the transition into Futurepack. Unlike traditional mods where you simply craft a machine to begin, Futurepack requires you to "learn" about the world around you. This is done through the E-Scanner, a handheld device that analyzes blocks and entities to unlock entries in your research book.

To begin, you must unlock the "Step into the Unknown" section. This requires you to find and scan specific environmental triggers. The E-Scanner is not just a research tool; it also functions as a utility device—for instance, it turns green when you are standing in a slime chunk, which is invaluable for early-game resource gathering.

### Essential Early Scanning Targets

| Target | Category Unlocked | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Piston** | Hydraulics | Shift + Right-click a placed Piston |
| **Neon Crystals** | Neon Technology | Found in underground crystal caves |
| **Redstone Ore** | Energy | Scan a natural Redstone Ore block |
| **Creeper** | Chemistry | Scan a live Creeper (be careful!) |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Neon crystals found in caves can be dangerous. Stepping on them deals damage, and breaking them creates significant particle effects that may cause temporary frame drops on lower-end systems.

Once these initial scans are complete, the "Scientific Curiosity" section of your guide will unlock, providing the blueprints for your first true technological workstations.

## Building the Foundation: Core Machines

After completing your initial scans, you must construct the Tech Table. This is a specialized workbench where almost all Futurepack-related items must be crafted. You cannot use a standard vanilla crafting table for these recipes. Following the **exoria wiki community guide** progression path, your next priority should be the Part Press and the Researcher.

### Core Workstation Overview

| Machine | Key Components | Primary Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Tech Table** | Iron, Wood | Required for all Futurepack crafting recipes. |
| **Part Press** | Piston, Iron, Coal | Converts iron ingots into **Iron Parts** for advanced machinery. |
| **Researcher** | Zinc, Glass, Paper, Piston | Processes research blueprints into usable technology. |
| **Industrial Furnace** | Tanks, Iron, Furnaces | Melts 3 items simultaneously using **Lava** as fuel. |

The Part Press requires fuel (usually coal) to operate. It is the only way to produce the "Iron Parts" and "Copper Coils" needed for the E-Scanner upgrades and the Researcher itself. Always keep a surplus of iron nearby, as the machine consumes it rapidly during the early expansion phase.

## The Research System and Blueprints

The Researcher is perhaps the most complex machine for new players to master. It doesn't just craft items; it processes your "ideas" into functional blueprints. When you scan a block with the E-Scanner, you often receive a Research Blueprint. However, these blueprints are often "unidentified" or require specific aspects to be selected.

When using the scanner on a block like an Industrial Furnace, you must select the correct scientific aspect (such as Thermodynamics). If you select the wrong aspect, you will receive a yellow X, indicating that your hypothesis was incorrect. Once you have a valid blueprint, you must place it in the Researcher along with the required "catalyst" items.

### Research Catalysts and Aspects

1.  **Thermodynamics**: Often requires **Furnaces** as a catalyst in the Researcher.
2.  **Logistics**: Requires **Hoppers** or **Chests** to complete the research.
3.  **Metallurgy**: Requires specific ores like **Bauxite** or **Zinc**.
4.  **Energy Generation**: Usually requires **Pistons** or **Redstone** components.

> **💡 Tip:** If the Researcher displays a yellow question mark, it means you have met the research requirements, but certain game conditions (like player level or previous research) have not been met yet.

## Advanced Resource Processing

As you progress through the **exoria wiki community guide** milestones, you will eventually reach the "Production" and "Logistics" tiers. The Industrial Furnace is a game-changer in 2026 for Exoria players. It processes three items at once and is significantly more efficient than standard furnaces, provided you have a steady supply of lava.

Lava can be difficult to manage in the early game, so many players choose to set up their initial industrial base near the Nether portal or use fluid pipes to transport lava from large underground pools. The Industrial Furnace also has a chance to produce secondary outputs like slag, which is used in later metallurgy recipes.

### Resource Priority List for 2026

*   **Bauxite**: Essential for aluminum production. Found in specific rock strata; keep your scanner active to identify it.
*   **Zinc**: Used heavily in the Researcher and for creating composite metals.
*   **Neon Sand**: Found in crystal caves. It is required for growing more Neon Crystals if you plan on staying in the tech tier for a long duration.

You can find more detailed item interactions and community-sourced data on the [Official Exoria CurseForge Page](https://www.curseforge.com/minecraft/modpacks/exoria) to stay updated on the latest patch changes.

## Logistics and Automation

The final step in mastering the mid-game is the "Item Logistics" tab. Once researched using hoppers and chests, you unlock access to advanced pipes and block breakers. These allow you to automate the input and output of your Part Press and Industrial Furnace.

In Exoria, automation is not just a luxury—it is a necessity for handling the sheer volume of materials required for end-game crafting. By connecting your Part Press to a chest via logistics pipes, you can ensure that you always have a steady supply of Iron Parts, which are the "screws and bolts" of the Futurepack world.

## FAQ

**Q: Why does my E-Scanner say "Game conditions not met" when I try to research a blueprint?**

A: This usually means you are missing a prerequisite research node. Check your book for any uncompleted entries in the "Basics" or "Scientific Curiosity" tabs. Some research also requires you to have specific items in your inventory or to have visited certain dimensions in the Exoria world.

**Q: Where is the best place to find Bauxite in Exoria?**

A: Bauxite is a rarer ore that typically spawns at mid-level elevations. According to the **exoria wiki community guide**, it is most commonly found in mountainous biomes or deep within the "Radon" cave systems. Use your E-Scanner to identify it from a distance through walls if you have the appropriate upgrades.

**Q: Can I automate the Researcher machine?**

A: While you can automate the input of catalyst items (like paper or furnaces), the actual selection of blueprints and the starting of the research process usually require manual interaction. However, once a research project is finished, the resulting technology is permanently unlocked in your database.

**Q: What should I do if my Neon Crystals stop growing?**

A: Neon Crystals require Neon Sand to grow. Ensure you haven't harvested all the sand in the area. It is recommended to leave at least one "seed" crystal on a block of Neon Sand to allow the cluster to regenerate over time. Avoid breaking the sand itself unless you are relocating your farm.
