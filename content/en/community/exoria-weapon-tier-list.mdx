## Weapon Locations and Tiered Progression

Progression in Exoria is divided into three distinct tiers. As you move from the starting town to the more dangerous regions, you must upgrade your gear to keep up with rising enemy HP pools.

### Tier 1: The Starter Kit
These weapons are found within the main town and are essential for your first few hours of gameplay.
*   **Sword & Dagger:** Located in the "Fighting Chance" shop near the blacksmith.
*   **Hammer:** Found in the Guild Hall, situated next to the Hammer Trainer.
*   **Spear:** This is located further out, near the Golem farming area. You will find it next to the Spear Trainer.

### Tier 2: The Coral Collection
Once you are strong enough to venture into the Goblin Camp, you can seek out the Tier 2 "Coral" weapons. These offer a significant stat boost over the starter gear.
*   **Location:** Navigate through the Goblin Camp toward the city near the Fist Trainer. A local blacksmith here sells the Tier 2 versions of the Hammer, Dagger, Sword, and Spear.

### Tier 3: The Sky Castle & Dungeons
The pinnacle of the **exoria weapon tier list** is found in the Sky Castle (Castellan). Reaching this area requires using the wooden elevator structure and navigating a high-level dungeon.

## Stats and Scaling: Maximizing Your Damage

Simply holding a high-tier weapon isn't enough; you must invest in the correct stats to unlock its full potential. In Exoria, your attributes directly influence how your weapon performs in the field.

1.  **Vitality:** Increases your total HP. Essential for Hammer and Great Sword users who need to trade hits with bosses.
2.  **Agility:** Boosts your stamina and unlocks the "Echo Step" talent. High Agility is mandatory for Spear and Dagger builds to maintain high mobility.
3.  **Psionics:** A unique stat that increases almost every other attribute by 1.35% per point. This is the "all-rounder" stat for players who want to maximize their overall efficiency.
4.  **Affinity:** Primarily for mana-based abilities, though some high-tier weapons require a baseline of Affinity to wield.

### Weapon Proficiency
As you use a specific weapon, your **Weapon Proficiency** will increase. This is a separate leveling system that grants raw damage bonuses. 
> **💡 Tip:** Focus on one weapon type early on. Spreading your proficiency across too many categories will leave you underpowered when you reach the Tier 3 bosses.

## Essential Crafting and Utility
Beyond your primary weapon, your survival in Exoria depends on secondary gear. You should prioritize crafting a **Glider** as soon as possible. It is the most effective tool for traversing the vertical landscapes of the Sky Castle and escaping dangerous encounters.

Furthermore, pay attention to your **Food Bar**. In Exoria, running out of food is a death sentence. Unlike other games where you might just lose health, in this world, your character will "explode" or die instantly if the food bar hits zero. Always carry cooked meals, which can be prepared at the cooking station near the Chief in the starting village.

### Dismantling and Refining
Don't throw away old gear or "broken" items like rings and golden cups. Take them to the dismantling trainer to break them down into crafting components. These materials are required to upgrade your Tier 2 weapons into their Tier 3 counterparts if you prefer crafting over dungeon crawling.

## Advanced Combat Tactics
When using items from the **exoria weapon tier list**, remember that combat is more than just clicking. 
*   **Stamina Management:** Every swing and dodge consumes the green bar. If you deplete it, you will be left vulnerable to a "Posture" break (the dark red bar).
*   **Factions:** Joining a faction like the High Sanctum (Faith) or The Fall (End) provides unique buffs that can complement your weapon choice. For example, some factions offer lifesteal or increased stamina regeneration.
*   **Trainers:** Once you reach certain proficiency milestones, visit your weapon trainer. They are scattered across the map (the Dagger trainer is near the food village at coordinates 195-210). They will teach you active skills like "Chain Bind" which can turn the tide of a fight.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best weapon for a beginner in Exoria?**

A: The **Spear** is widely considered the best beginner weapon. Its long range allows you to keep enemies at a distance, reducing the amount of damage you take while you learn enemy attack patterns. It also has a very high attack speed, making it forgiving if you miss a strike.

**Q: How do I get Tier 3 weapons?**

A: Tier 3 weapons are found in the Sky Castle dungeon (Castellan). You must take the wooden elevator up, navigate through a dark dungeon area using a light source, and find the secret weapon racks. The Hammer has its own dedicated armor and weapon room, while the Spear, Sword, and Dagger are grouped together deeper in the ruins.

**Q: Why is my damage so low even with a Tier 2 weapon?**

A: Damage in Exoria is heavily tied to your **Weapon Proficiency** and your stat points. If you haven't used that weapon type much, your proficiency will be low. Additionally, ensure you are putting points into Agility (for fast weapons) or Psionics (for general scaling) to boost your output.

**Q: What happens if my food bar reaches zero?**

A: You will die automatically and immediately. Always keep an eye on your hunger meter and carry cooked food items. You can obtain food from the village located near the Dagger trainer.

**Q: Is the Hammer viable in the current exoria weapon tier list?**

A: While the Hammer is lower on the tier list due to its slow speed, it is highly viable for players who use heavy armor and focus on "Posture" damage. It is excellent for breaking the guard of shielded enemies and bosses, though it requires much better timing than the Spear or Sword.
