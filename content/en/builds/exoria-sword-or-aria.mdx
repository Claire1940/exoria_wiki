## The Sword Class: Master of Close-Quarters Combat

The Sword class in Exoria is designed for players who crave high-intensity, reactive gameplay. Unlike traditional RPG swordplay, this class emphasizes "flow." With the recent addition of dash cancellation, the Sword class allows players to break out of animations to dodge incoming attacks or extend combos, making it one of the most fluid classes available in 2026.

At launch, the Sword class features a robust move set consisting of 8 to 12 unique skills. These skills are not static; they evolve based on your character's race. For instance, non-demon races have access to an "Upgraded" sword form that features distinct visual effects and transformation cutscenes.

## The Aria Class: The Power of the Mage

If you prefer to stay on the fringes of the battlefield while raining down destruction, the Aria class is your go-to option. Described as a "powerful mage class," the Aria focuses on incantations and elemental control. While the Sword class relies on physical dexterity, the Aria relies on positioning and cooldown management.

The Aria class is particularly effective in group play, where they can provide covering fire for Sword users. However, solo Aria players must be wary of the game’s aggressive AI, as they lack the immediate defensive parries inherent to the blade-wielding counterparts.

## The Purity System and Karma

A defining feature of Exoria in 2026 is the Purity Bar. This bar represents your character's karma and is directly influenced by the quests you accept and the choices you make during dialogue and combat encounters. Whether you are using the **exoria sword or aria** class, your purity level will dictate your standing in the world.

*   **High Purity (Good):** Often leads to "Holy" or "Exorcist" style skill variations.
*   **Low Purity (Evil):** Leans toward "Demonic" or "Corrupted" power sets.

This system ensures that two players using the same class can have vastly different experiences. A "Low Purity" Sword user might utilize shadow-infused slashes, while a "High Purity" Aria might focus on light-based purification spells.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Your choices are permanent. Before taking a quest that significantly lowers your purity, ensure that the "Evil" path aligns with the specific build you are trying to create.

## Race and Transformation Mechanics

Demon races, conversely, tap into raw, chaotic power that often sacrifices defense for overwhelming offensive capabilities.

## Combat Mechanics: Dash Cancellation

One of the most praised additions to the Exoria combat system is dash cancellation. This mechanic allows players to interrupt their own skill animations by performing a dash. This is vital for the Sword class, as it allows for "frame-perfect" dodging.

For Aria users, dash cancellation is used primarily for kiting—maintaining a safe distance from enemies while continuing to cast spells. Learning the rhythm of your class's animations is key to surviving the higher-level dungeons found in the later stages of the game.

### Progression Steps for New Players

1.  **Select Class:** Choose between the melee-focused Sword or the magic-heavy Aria.
2.  **Monitor Purity:** Pay attention to your Karma bar during the first few levels.
3.  **Practice Cancels:** Spend time in the training area mastering the dash cancel timing.
4.  **Race Synergy:** Align your race (Demon/Non-Demon) with your desired transformation style.
5.  **Upgrade Skills:** Focus on upgrading your base move set to unlock the 8-12 unique skills.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I switch between the exoria sword or aria classes later in the game?**

A: Currently, class selection is tied to your character slot. To experience both classes, most players create a secondary character. There is no "multi-classing" system currently implemented in the 2026 build.

**Q: Does the Sword class have more skills than the Aria class?**

A: Both classes are designed to have a balanced number of skills, ranging from 8 to 12 unique moves at launch. While the Sword class has been showcased more extensively in recent progress videos, the Aria class is designed to be equally deep in its mechanical complexity.

**Q: How does the Purity bar affect my damage?**

A: The Purity bar doesn't necessarily increase raw damage numbers, but it changes the *type* of damage you deal and the utility of your skills. High purity might add healing or stun effects to your Aria spells, while low purity might add bleed or burn effects to your Sword strikes.

**Q: Is Exoria available on mobile devices?**

A: Yes, Exoria is fully optimized for Mobile, PC, and Consoles. The UI and combat mechanics, including dash cancellation, have been tailored to ensure a smooth experience across all supported platforms in 2026.
